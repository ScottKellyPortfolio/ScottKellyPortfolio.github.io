About Me

I am a fan of tech. Period. Whether it's at work learning new technologies, languages, tools, or at home reading about the latest gadgets, smartphones, computer components, whatever. That's what inspires me. I love learning new, techy, nerdy things.

Professionally, I'm a software engineer/software developer. I specialize in 'back-end' and Microsoft technolocies, such as .NET Framework, .NET Core, C#, SQL Server, but I am pretty well rounded in other technologies as well. I have built my own REST APIs in my own personal projects, consumed (mmmm, tasty) a myriad of other APIs, mainly REST, and have built web services and REST APIs based on existing platforms. I personally quite enjoy building, testing, and working with APIs (again, mainly REST). My main interests in back-end work are building de-coupled systems with API integrations, data analysis, and optimizing code. I am always wanting to learn more with algorithms, lower level operations and such, but don't have much experience with them. I find those challenges difficult and rewarding and would love an opportunity to learn more.

I also have experience in front-end work and have done a great deal with HTML, CSS, Javascript (JS), jQuery, some Angular and React. I enjoy trying to learn new front-end frameworks and seeing what works well for me and what I find the most useful. While my focus isn't necessarily front-end work, I'd be lying if I said I didn't enjoy it or I'm not competent.

Personally, I was born and raised in Brownsburg, Indiana, where I still currently reside. I was an early-on career-changer. My full-time working career started in the drag racing industry where I worked for the most succesful drag racing team ever, John Force Racing. I had had an interest in cars and throughout highschool I had been involved in programs at school with super high mileage cars and misc engineering courses, and it was a dream jon for me. I worked there for nearly 4 years, and in my time there, I had worked my way onto a team as a mechanic working on one of the nitro funny cars. As cool as that job was, I came to the conclusion that it wasn't sustainable for me.

This is when my enjoyment of tech took me in a new direction. I had heard of a new program from Eleven Fifty Academy that was originally was intended to be a skill-up class for existing developers. They were modifying it to be a ground-up coding course for people with zero prior coding experience. I figured this was a great opportunity for me to see if coding was right for me. I took a weekend intro course, and the rest is pretty much history. I was hooked nearly immediately, signed up for the course, and that has out me down the path I'm currently on. While I learned SO much from my time racing and am very grateful for having had it, I am equally as thankful for the opportunity I had to make the career-change when I did. It's no going back for me, I've found what I'm passionate about and that's that.

Projects

Weight And Balance Calculator

Design, Development

The idea for this application came from my father, who pilots private charter jets. The purpose of the app, is that it makes it simple to calculate the weight and balance of an aircraft before it flies. It uses built in information for different aircraft to make it simple to give your weights as inputs, and it will tell you if the aircraft is safe to fly for the weights the user input. At this point in time, it is still a work in progress, but is close to being finished.

The Challenge

Simplify calculating the various weights of small aircraft to make calculating the weight and balance easier. Make it easy to use and make it mobile friendly so as to avoid needing a desktop or laptop.

The Solution

A responsive web application that can be used on mobile devices to increase ease and productivity. Replaces the need to use a computer or desktop application.

The Result

Still to come! This is a work in progress project as I am the only person working on it. Unfortunately because it is a personal project, it takes the backseat for more pressing issues.

NoteJar

Design, Development

The Challenge

This was a project I did during the 9 week Accelerated Learning Program at the Eleven Fifty Academy. We were instructed to take and existing project, a very simple note taking CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) application, and enhance functionality, design, and features of the app.

The Solution

The existing project only involved notes like the second screenshot. We decided to use a bootstrap theme to change the basic color and look of the website. We completely changed the home screen to include a picture of a cat in wonder, but we also added many quotes and testimonials from our classmates. We also added a contact page which was functional and forwarded emails sent through the form to me as well as a calender on the note page.
